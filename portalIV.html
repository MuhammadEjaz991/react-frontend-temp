EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

CBRE's Mid-Atlantic Debt & Structured Finance Group is presenting Portals IV, a luxury multifamily development project located in the vibrant Southwest Waterfront neighborhood of Washington, DC. The project, developed by Republic, consists of 363 new luxury apartments and 250 parking spaces. It is the final addition to the Portals Complex, which includes office buildings, luxury apartments, and the Mandarin Oriental Hotel. The Project is fully entitled and construction is scheduled to begin in June 2022. The total project budget is approximately $222 million, and the Sponsor is seeking non-recourse construction financing of 65% loan-to-cost, or approximately $144 million in loan proceeds. With its prime location, the Property offers stunning panoramic views of the Potomac River and Washington, DC's historic landmarks, including the Jefferson Memorial, Washington Monument, Tidal Basin, and U.S. Capitol. Residents will also have easy access to hundreds of high-end retailers, restaurants, and premier museums in the area.

EXPLOSIVE GROWTH ACCELERATED BY DEEP-ROOTED SUPPLY/DEMAND IMBALANCE

The Washington, DC region is experiencing explosive growth in its property market, driven by a deep-rooted supply and demand imbalance. Despite facing economic challenges in 2020 due to the COVID-19 pandemic, the region is forecasted to bounce back strongly, with regional output expected to grow at an average annual rate of 3.0% over the next four years. The population of the area is young, highly educated, and has a high earning power, making it an attractive market for property sales.
Additionally, rising home prices in the region, with the median home price currently at $700,000 compared to the national average of $300,000, have led to more people choosing to rent rather than own a home. This has resulted in a greater demand for apartments, with vacancy rates dropping and the multifamily market experiencing one of its strongest years of demand on record in 2021. As a property selling expert, this explosive growth and demand in the Washington, DC market present a lucrative opportunity for selling properties and capitalizing on the high demand and limited supply.
